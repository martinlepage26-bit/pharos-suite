# Govern AI Principles Alignment

Date: 2026-03-06

This note records the principle-level alignment between the public Govern AI site and the deeper COM-AUR / InfraFabric architecture work.

## Core principle

Govern AI should not present a stronger public posture than the evidence architecture beneath it can support.

That means the site should consistently communicate five things:

1. governance documentation must derive from evidence
2. decision rights and thresholds must be explicit
3. pressure from audit, procurement, and oversight is normal, not exceptional
4. legal or certification claims are not made where they are not justified
5. product capability claims should narrow when infrastructure or evidence is still maturing

## Public-facing implications

The public site can confidently claim:

- legible governance under scrutiny
- evidence-ready documentation
- review structures, decision rights, controls, and operating cadence
- support for procurement, audit, and vendor review preparation

The public site should avoid implying:

- certification
- guaranteed compliance outcomes
- infrastructure maturity beyond current evidence
- public readiness of internal modules before their review surfaces are stable

## This pass

This alignment pass changed copy only.

It did not change:

- layout
- color
- typography
- spacing system
- navigation structure
- interaction design

The purpose was to bring the wording on `Home`, `About`, `Services`, and `Connect` into closer agreement with the evidence-first, fail-closed posture established in the COM-AUR v3 specification.
