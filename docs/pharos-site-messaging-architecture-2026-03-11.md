# PHAROS Site Messaging Architecture

## Purpose

This note translates the Recursive Deterministic AI Governance material into a public-facing website structure that fits the existing PHAROS position.

The goal is not to rebrand PHAROS as a generic compliance consultancy, a small-business advisory, or an investor-facing AI startup. The goal is to strengthen the current position:

- legible governance under pressure
- evidence before rhetoric
- decision rights, thresholds, and review-ready documentation
- method depth without front-door abstraction

## Positioning Guardrails

### Keep

- `PHAROS - Legible AI Governance`
- `Deterministic AI governance under pressure`
- pressure-source framing: procurement, audit, vendor review, executive oversight
- evidence, thresholds, decision rights, controls, provenance, review packets
- method as a way to turn ethical and governance concerns into inspectable workflow controls

### Integrate Carefully

- bounded corpus
- recursive transformation
- diagnostic recursion
- semantic drift
- normative compression
- agency redistribution
- provenance fidelity
- operational controls such as review gates, escalation triggers, and provenance rules

These belong mainly on the method page, research page, and selected service language. They should support the offer, not become the offer.

### Do Not Lead With Publicly

- `SMB compliance services`
- `Governance-as-a-Service`
- `shared latent space` as front-door language
- investor language such as market size, exit potential, acquisition, margins
- public fixed pricing unless the commercial model changes deliberately

Those frames weaken the current authority signal or belong in a separate investor or strategy deck.

## Core Message Structure

PHAROS should keep speaking in this order:

1. Pressure
2. Legibility problem
3. Control response
4. Evidence output
5. Next step

In plain language:

- When scrutiny arrives, teams need governance that can be followed.
- PHAROS turns that pressure into explicit thresholds, decision rights, and review-ready evidence.
- The method matters because it produces inspectable controls rather than abstract ethical claims.

### 3. Method

Current route:

- `/about/conceptual-method`

Recommended public framing:

- Label: `Conceptual method`
- Title: `A conceptual method for integrating ethics into AI models`
- Bridge line:
  `This method turns ethical concern into bounded inputs, recursive comparison, control extraction, and deployment-time safeguards.`

Public section order:

1. What the method does
2. Four plain-language stages
3. Deeper analytic layers
4. Control extraction
5. Failure doctrine
6. Glossary

Recommended plain-language structure:

#### Stage 1. Bound the material

Public copy:
`Start with a clearly delimited corpus. Define what enters the workflow, what it is for, how sensitive it is, and what can and cannot be done with it.`

#### Stage 2. Run recursive comparison

Public copy:
`Pass the bounded material through multiple AI cycles, prompts, and formats. Use repetition to see where meaning stays stable, where it drifts, and where important distinctions collapse.`

#### Stage 3. Diagnose the pattern

Public copy:
`Treat outputs as AI-mediated artifacts rather than simple answers. Compare them for semantic drift, normative compression, agency redistribution, and provenance loss.`

#### Stage 4. Extract controls

Public copy:
`Turn repeated patterns into workflow controls: review gates, escalation triggers, provenance rules, logging requirements, and deployment safeguards.`

Terms safe to introduce on this page:

- bounded corpus
- recursive transformation
- diagnostic recursion
- AI-mediated artifacts
- semantic drift
- normative compression
- agency redistribution
- provenance fidelity

Term to use carefully:

- `shared latent space`

If used at all, it should be translated immediately:

`Here, "shared latent space" means the comparative field in which outputs from different models, prompts, and passes can be examined for recurring distortions and stable patterns.`

## Public Copy Themes To Reuse

These lines fit the current PHAROS rationale and can be reused across pages:

- `Legibility is not branding. It is whether another reviewer can follow the decision.`
- `The aim is not abstract ethics alone. The aim is inspectable workflow control.`
- `Good governance narrows claims before scrutiny does it for you.`
- `What matters is not whether a team says it cares about AI ethics. What matters is whether the workflow shows who decides, what evidence exists, and where escalation begins.`
- `Recursive testing matters because it reveals where meaning drifts, where authority gets reassigned, and where provenance starts to degrade.`
- `The output is not commentary alone. The output is a control surface a reviewer can inspect.`

## Terms To Avoid Or Translate

Avoid on the home page and main service hero:

- shared latent space
- symbolic instability
- classificatory habit
- sociotechnical recursion
- normative compression

Better public translations:

- `where meaning drifts`
- `where nuance collapses`
- `where the model starts assigning authority in the wrong way`
- `where provenance gets lost`
- `where review needs to intervene`

## Pricing And Commercial Language

Public website guidance:

- keep `scoped after initial review`
- keep `targeted review`, `multi-system cycle`, and `follow-through`
- do not publish investor logic, market-size claims, or exit language
- do not anchor the public identity in `SMB` if the actual posture is scrutiny-grade governance

If a pricing page is ever expanded, it should still be framed around:

- scope
- evidence condition
- system count
- review burden
- remediation complexity

not around startup-style revenue framing.

## Bottom Line

The recursive method strengthens PHAROS when it is presented as the disciplined way the practice converts ethical and governance ambiguity into inspectable controls.

It weakens PHAROS when it is presented as:

- a generic compliance offer
- a small-business website category
- a theory-heavy front door
- an investor narrative disguised as public positioning

Publicly, the hierarchy should remain:

- legibility
- scrutiny
- thresholds
- decision rights
- evidence
- method

not the other way around.
