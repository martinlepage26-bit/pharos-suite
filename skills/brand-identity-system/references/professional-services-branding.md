# Professional Services Branding

Use this for governance, policy, legal, compliance, advisory, research, and other trust-heavy categories.

## What serious brands must signal

- clarity
- restraint
- judgment
- competence
- inspection-readiness
- document compatibility

## What to avoid

- startup clichés
- ornamental futurism
- generic AI gradients without strategic purpose
- noisy symbolism
- luxury codes that imply exclusivity more than trust

## Trust is not the same as warmth

A serious brand can be calm without becoming soft.

Trust in these categories usually comes from:
- legibility
- consistency
- measured authority
- system behavior across proposals, slides, reports, and web pages

## Restraint rule

When the offer carries institutional or regulatory weight:
- reduce visual novelty
- increase typographic control
- make proof easy to find
- design for long-form reading, not only splash-page impact

## Common failure modes

- looks like a startup instead of an authority
- looks like a law firm without enough distinctiveness
- looks elegant but collapses in documents
- looks futuristic but signals weak judgment
- looks minimal because it lacks a point of view
