# Reviewer Response Letter Template

Use this as a starting template for point-by-point revision letters. Replace
every bracketed placeholder and tailor the level of detail to the actual
comments and revisions.

## Opening

Dear [Editor Name] and Reviewers,

Thank you for the careful reading of our manuscript, "[Manuscript Title]," and
for the thoughtful comments. We have revised the manuscript substantially in
response to this feedback. Below we address each point in turn and indicate
where changes appear in the revised manuscript.

## Revision Summary

In this revision, we [briefly summarize the main changes in 2-4 sentences].
These changes are intended to clarify [argument/method/findings], strengthen
[evidence/framing/organization], and address the concerns raised by the
reviewers.

## Overall Revision Stance

Use this short bridge when several comments point to the same deeper issue.

[In response to the reviews, we made one central revision move: briefly name
the shift in argument, method, framing, or evidence. Then explain how that
change shaped the manuscript as a whole, not just individual sentences.]

## Point-by-Point Responses

### Reviewer 1

Comment 1: [Quote or fair paraphrase of the comment]

Response: Thank you for this comment. We have revised the manuscript to
[describe the change concretely]. In the revised manuscript, this change
appears in [section or page number if available]. This revision clarifies
[what changed in the argument, framing, evidence, or presentation].

Comment 2: [Quote or fair paraphrase of the comment]

Response: Thank you for raising this issue. We now [describe the revision]. We
made this change in [section or page number]. Our goal was to
[explain why this change addresses the concern].

### Reviewer 2

Comment 1: [Quote or fair paraphrase of the comment]

Response: Thank you for this helpful suggestion. We revised the manuscript by
[describe the change]. This appears in [section or page number]. We believe
this revision improves [clarity/methodological transparency/argument
precision].

## Respectful Decline Template

Use only when the suggestion will not be adopted in full:

Thank you for this thoughtful suggestion. We agree that [acknowledge the value
or concern]. We did not adopt this recommendation exactly as proposed because
[brief, professional reason tied to scope, evidence, method, or journal
constraints]. To address the underlying concern, we instead [describe the
alternative revision]. This change appears in [section or page number].

## Closing

We appreciate the reviewers' and editor's time and feedback. We hope the
revisions have strengthened the manuscript and addressed the concerns raised.

Sincerely,

[Author Name or Team]
