# Manuscript Readiness Checklist

Load this file when the user wants a publication-readiness assessment, a
submission checklist, or a triage of what still blocks peer review.

## Core Argument

- Is the governing claim explicit within the opening section?
- Is the scope bounded by object, site, period, population, corpus, or case?
- Does the paper explain why the argument matters for a defined field or
  debate?
- Does the contribution stay proportionate to the evidence?

## Structure

- Does each section do a distinct job?
- Does the introduction identify the problem, gap, claim, and roadmap when
  useful?
- Does the literature review synthesize debates instead of summarizing sources
  one by one?
- Does the discussion interpret findings rather than repeat results?
- Does the conclusion sharpen the payoff instead of only restating the
  introduction?

## Methods and Evidence

- Is the research question aligned with the method?
- Are the data, corpus, or materials clearly described?
- Is the sampling or selection logic explicit?
- Is the analytic procedure explained well enough for a reviewer to follow?
- Are limitations acknowledged without undermining the full paper?
- Do major claims match the strength of the evidence provided?

## Concepts and Framing

- Are key concepts defined before they do analytic work?
- Does theory clarify the analysis instead of decorate it?
- Are causal, interpretive, or comparative claims phrased at the right level
  of certainty?

## Citation Discipline

- Are all non-common-knowledge claims supported by real, relevant sources?
- Are any quotations missing page numbers or verification?
- Are there places where multiple repetitive citations should be synthesized?
- Are placeholders such as `[citation needed]` or `[verify source]` still
  present?

## Presentation

- Is the title specific about topic, angle, and context?
- Does the abstract state problem, object, method, finding, and contribution?
- Are paragraphs organized around `claim -> evidence -> analysis ->
  implication`?
- Are ambiguous pronouns, inflated language, or filler transitions still
  present?

## Submission Triage

Use this rating scale when summarizing readiness:

- Ready to submit: only light polishing or formatting remains.
- Revise before submission: argument is viable, but one or more sections still
  need substantive tightening.
- Not yet submission-ready: the central issue is argument alignment, evidence
  sufficiency, or method-claim fit rather than polish.

When reporting readiness, name the top three blockers and the smallest set of
revisions that would materially improve the manuscript.
