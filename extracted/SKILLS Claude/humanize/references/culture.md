# Calibrate For Culture

Use this file when a rule must work across countries, institutions, or team cultures.

## Keep Stable

Keep these stable across versions unless the user explicitly changes them:

- the actual obligation
- the owner
- the timing
- the threshold
- the consequence

Change only the framing, directness, examples, and relationship signals.

## High Context Vs Low Context

### Low-context settings

Examples often include the United States, Germany, and Australia.

- Make the rule explicit.
- Spell out timing, ownership, and steps.
- Humanize through clarity, not vagueness.

### High-context settings

Examples often include Japan, Arab contexts, and Brazil.

- Leave room for relationship-sensitive phrasing when appropriate.
- Avoid sounding insulting by over-explaining obvious social expectations.
- Humanize through respect, context, and face-saving structure.

## Power Distance

### Lower power distance

- Avoid cold hierarchy when collaboration matters.
- Excessively formal tone can trigger reactance.
- Peer or coach voice often works better.

### Higher power distance

- Avoid language so casual that it sounds weak or optional.
- Firm, respectful authority usually works better than over-familiarity.

## Individualism Vs Collectivism

### More individualist audiences

- Emphasize personal responsibility and direct benefit.
- Example: `Please complete this step to protect your access and your account.`

### More collectivist audiences

- Emphasize group safety, trust, and shared responsibility.
- Example: `We complete this step to protect the team and keep the process fair.`

## Softening And Strengthening

- Use `must` or `need to` when the action is required.
- Use `should` for guidance only.
- Use `may` for permission, not obligation.
- If translation turns the sentence into a weak suggestion, strengthen it.

## Translator Test

Ask:

- If this sentence is translated, will it still sound mandatory?
- If not, replace soft language with firmer wording.

Goal:

- be kind
- be clear
- stay enforceable
